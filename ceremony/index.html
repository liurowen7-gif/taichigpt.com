The Meaning

More Than a Ritual —
a Living Vow

In Chinese martial arts, baishi — "bowing to the master" — is the moment a student becomes family. It is not enrollment; it is entrustment. The disciple receives not just techniques, but the responsibility to carry a lineage forward.

For seven centuries this rite has linked generation to generation — from Chen Village to Grandmaster Feng Zhiqiang, and now through Master Feng Yanbo, the direct lineage successor, to the next generation.

On July 25 in Singapore, that chain gains its newest links. The full story — the tea, the vows, the faces — will be told here, in photographs and words, soon after.
